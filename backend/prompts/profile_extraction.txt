SYSTEM:
You are extracting a structured personality/behavioral profile from interview
transcripts to create a digital twin that can simulate this person convincingly.

PRIORITIES (in order of importance):
1. Conditional behavioral rules — specific if/then patterns grounded in quotes
2. Narrative memories — stories, anecdotes, personal moments that reveal character
3. Voice/style capture — how they talk, hedge, explain, their characteristic phrases
4. Tensions/contradictions — where stated values conflict with actual behavior
5. Exemplar quotes — the most representative things they said in their own words
6. Trait dimensions — OCEAN and preference axes as secondary summary, not primary goal

DEVELOPER:
Completed modules: {completed_modules}
All interview turns (by module):
{all_module_turns}

TASK:
Extract a comprehensive structured profile. Focus on capturing the person as a
believable individual with specific patterns, not as a set of abstract trait scores.

EXTRACTION INSTRUCTIONS:

behavioral_rules (5-15 rules):
- Extract conditional "when X happens, I do Y" patterns from their answers
- Each rule MUST include a source_quote — a direct fragment from the interview
- Cover all domains mentioned in the interview (decision_making, spending, lifestyle, etc.)
- Prefer specific rules ("When a purchase is over $500, I compare at least 3 options")
  over vague ones ("I'm careful with money")

voice_signature:
- tone_descriptors: 3-5 adjectives for how they communicate (e.g. "self-deprecating, measured, enthusiastic")
- characteristic_phrases: 3-5 actual phrases they use repeatedly (e.g. "honestly", "the thing is")
- hedging_style: how they qualify uncertain statements (e.g. "adds 'I think' before opinions")
- explanation_style: do they use stories, examples, data, abstract reasoning?
- formality_level: casual / mixed / formal

tensions (0-5):
- Find places where the person's stated values conflict with their behavior or other statements
- Include the two domains involved and how (if at all) they reconcile the contradiction
- Only include genuine tensions, not minor inconsistencies

narrative_memories (3-8):
- Find stories, anecdotes, or personal moments the person shared during the interview
- These are NOT traits — they are specific memories or experiences that reveal character
- Include the emotional tone (warm, conflicted, proud, anxious, etc.)
- Explain why this memory matters for understanding the person
- Tag the source module

exemplar_quotes (5-10):
- The most representative, vivid, or revealing things they said in their own words
- Prefer specific and surprising over generic
- These should capture the person's voice and values

OCEAN estimates (secondary):
- Fill in as a compact summary derived from behavioral evidence
- These are supporting data, not the main extraction target
